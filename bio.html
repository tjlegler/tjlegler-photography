Artist Biography

TJ is a multidisciplinary photographer whose work merges light, movement, and environment into visionary visual experiences. Beginning his creative journey in Mississippi, TJ was featured in the PBS broadcast Mississippi Roads in the segment “Sparks of Creation”, where his lightpainting process using steel wool and long-exposure techniques was showcased at the Mississippi Museum of Art.

After relocating to Colorado, he reemerged post-pandemic with a series of art shows across the state and was selected for the Rural Art Show Rural/Urban Exchange, where his work highlighted the relationship between humans, landscape, and motion.

TJ’s photography evolved into immersive lightpainting and cinematic portraiture, eventually giving rise to The Neon Lounge — a collaborative, evolving art installation integrating live projection, mixed digital textures, and long-exposure portrait creation. As a resident artist, he became a staple across Front Range venues including Cervantes, The Black Box, Junkyard Social Club, Vali Soul, and multiple Colorado music and arts festivals.

In addition to his creative installations, TJ’s fine art practice includes high-end metal prints, gallery-quality framed work, and expansive landscape photography across the American West. His involvement with Matador Network led to a featured tourism photography package, further expanding his creative reach.

Today, TJ continues to explore the boundaries of light, time, and perception — producing deeply atmospheric imagery that blends mystical storytelling with technical precision. His nomadic approach to artmaking fuels an ever-growing body of work grounded in freedom, exploration, and experiential creativity.
